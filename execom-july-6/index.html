Section Three · Tagline Options

Two finalists.
Let's hear them out loud.

The list is down to two. So instead of judging words on a page, we've placed each line inside real, everyday moments — and tested Bisaya versions, because this isn't translation. It's checking whether the feeling still lands the way people actually speak in Visayas and Mindanao.

Route 1

"Laging Kasama"

Consistency & familiarity — Julie's is already part of the daily routine. Breakfast, baon, merienda. There with you, always.
Route 2

"Tara, Kain Tayo"

An invitation, not a statement — the "tara" moment that sparks a shared meal. Bonding, conversation, togetherness.
The question isn't which sounds better — it's which one you can hear in real life, across the country.

Tagline · One refinement
New this week

"Tara, Merienda Tayo"

One honest catch with "kain" and "mangaon": they can read as a full meal. "Merienda" keeps the exact same invitation — but names the occasion Julie's actually owns. Bread, pastries, coffee breaks, afternoon snacks.

Same warmth, same "tara" — more ownable for a bakeshop. Two further product-led lines are held in reserve if the room wants to see them.
